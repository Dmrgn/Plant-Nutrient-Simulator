Description:

  • White molecules represent nutrients and minerals in the soil
  • Brown molecules represent other molecules in the rhizosphere
  • The cell's membrane is semi-permeable, meaning only nutrients are able to move freely through it
  • Overtime, and as the amount of nutrients increase, the concentration inside the cell will increase and stablize such that the concentration of molecules outside of the cell balances with the concentration of molecules inside of the cell
  • Even when nutrients enter the system on the opposite side of the cell, an equilibrium will eventually be established
  • In real life, plants will consume and transport nutrients away. This will lower the concentration of nutrients inside of the cell and allow for more nutrients to enter the cell

Chemistry:

  • This equilibrium is called passive diffusion and can be represented loosely by the following equation:
  • Ninside ⇆ Noutside
  • Since the concentration of molecules inside of the cell will be equal to the concentration of molecules outside of the cell at equilibruim, the Keq is equal to 1
  • Following Le Chatelier's Principle:
    • When there is a higher concentration of molecules outside of the cell, the equilibrium will shift left. This means that there will be more molecules that enter the cell than molecules that leave the cell
    • When there is a lower concentration of molecules inside of the cell, the equilibrium will shift right. This means that there will be more molecules that leave the cell than molecules that enter the cell
  • In many cases, this passive diffusion force is not efficient enough to sustain a plant's needs. Some nutrients are found in much higher concentrations inside of the cell, and so active transportation methods are required

Application:

  • In soybean plants, iron deficiency is a critical problem which influences agricultural efficiency
  • Soybean plants are unable to utilize the insoluble Iron(III) Oxide which is present in most soils
  • In order to access this iron source, soybean plants release hydrogen ions. This causes the pH of the soil to lower, which causes the oxide to become freed from the iron:
    • Fe2O3 + 6H+ -> 2Fe3+ + 3H2O
  • Iron(III) is still unusable though, so the soybean uses a membrane-bound reductase to give an electron and create Iron(II):
    • Fe3+ + e- -> Fe2+
  • Finally, the Iron(II) can be absorbed by a divalent metal transporter in the cell membrane.
  • In terms of equilibrium:
    • When the cell transports iron elsewhere, or is iron deficient, it signals for more divalent metal transporters to be created on the cell membrane. This causes more Iron(II) to be transported into the cell
    • When the cell has elevated concentrations of iron or other metal ions, less hydrogen ions are released into the soil. This causes the concentration of usable Iron(II) ions outside of the cell to decrease overtime (as it is brought into the cell but not replaced)
